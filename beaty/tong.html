Optimization in Data Science: Bridging the gap between theory and practice

A talk by Dr. Weitian Tong

Monday, March 4th, 1200 -- 1200 AES 200K

Scheduling plays an important role in data science, as scheduling is the allocation of various resources for processing the data with one or more optimization goals. Common applications can be found in the manufacturing industries, as well for airline and package delivery companies. In addition, complex scheduling problems frequently arise in the increasingly popular Internet-of-Things (IoT) and cloud computing, where computational recourses need to be assigned to achieve the overall computation efficiency. In this presentation, an interesting scheduling problem, Parallel Multi-stage Flow-shops, will be introduced. In the parallel k-stage flow-shops problem, we are given m identical k-stage flow-shops and a set of jobs. Each job can be processed by any one of the flow-shops but switching between flow-shops is not allowed. The objective is to minimize the makespan, which is the finishing time of the last job. This problem is motivated by an application in cloud computing. Due to its NP-hardness, we concentrated on its approximability and proposed the current best approximation algorithms for two cases.
